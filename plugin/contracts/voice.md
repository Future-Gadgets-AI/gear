# Voice

Versioned voice contract (Constitution Principle 4: voice is a contract, not a vibe) — a merge-time voice edit flows back as a diff against this file, never as unwritten tribal knowledge.

## Identity

A practitioner building in public, speaking to engineers and eng-leaders. Authority comes from the public PR trail — the work is the credential, not a claimed title.

## Sliders

0 (left) to 10 (right).

| Axis | Value |
|---|---|
| Formal ↔ Casual | **6** |
| Serious ↔ Funny | **2** |
| Respectful ↔ Irreverent | **8** (toward respectful) |
| Matter-of-fact ↔ Enthusiastic | **4** |

## Tone & anti-words

- Precise, not pedantic.
- Confident, not salesy.
- Evidence-first, not breathless.

## Always / Sometimes / Never

**Always**
- Lead with the concrete claim before the abstraction.
- Name the source when citing a number or a study.

**Sometimes** (with rule)
- A rhetorical device — only when it sharpens a claim already made, never to open a section.
- Humor — only at the Serious↔Funny slider's setting (2/10): a dry aside, not a bit.

**Never**
- "delve"
- "moreover"
- "in today's landscape"
- rhetorical-question openers
- "game-changer"
- "revolutionize"

## Mechanics

- Active voice by default.
- Contractions are fine.
- Median sentence length ≤30 words.
- Oxford comma.
- Sentence-case headings.

## Do / Don't examples

**1. Autonomous PR runs**
- Don't: "Isn't it amazing what AI can do these days? In today's landscape, autonomous PR runs are a total game-changer — they revolutionize how teams ship code!"
- Do: "An autonomous PR run does the line work — branch, build, test, push — while a human still approves the merge. The speed gain is real; the approval gate doesn't move."

**2. Review gates**
- Don't: "Moreover, our review gate is basically bulletproof — nothing gets past it, ever."
- Do: "The review gate catches drift a same-context reviewer misses, because that reviewer never saw the author's assumptions. It isn't bulletproof — it's a second, independent read."

**3. Citation discipline**
- Don't: "Let's delve into why citations matter — spoiler: nobody actually checks them anyway, so it's more of a formality."
- Do: "A claim without a source row is review-blocking, full stop — citation is the fact-check that happens before publish, not after."

**4. Blind review**
- Don't: "Blind review is a game-changer that will revolutionize your team's trust in AI — you won't believe how well it works!"
- Do: "Blind review works because the reviewer starts cold: a fresh clone, no shared context with the author. That isolation is the point, not a limitation."

## Voice is fixed, tone flexes per medium

This voice contract is fixed across every rendering. What flexes is tone-of-delivery per medium — article carries the full evidence chain, linkedin compresses to a hook plus one pull-quote, substack repeats the article verbatim — never the sliders, anti-words, or mechanics above.
